Photosynthesis: Turning Light into Life

Photosynthesis is the fundamental process by which green plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy. Using chlorophyll pigments, these organisms capture sunlight and transform carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen. This not only fuels their growth but also releases the oxygen we breathe.

Key Steps

  1. Light Reactions: Sunlight is absorbed, splitting water molecules and generating ATP and NADPH.
  2. Calvin Cycle: ATP and NADPH drive the conversion of CO2 into glucose.

Why It Matters

Photosynthesis underpins most food webs and regulates atmospheric CO2. Understanding it is crucial for agriculture, climate science, and renewable energy research.
